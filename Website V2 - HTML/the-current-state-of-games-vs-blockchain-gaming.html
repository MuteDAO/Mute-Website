The Current State of Games vs. Blockchain Gaming

Blockchain technology has been disrupting every sector, and it is no surprise to see it evolving and transforming the gaming ecosystem. Blockchain gaming has been making a splash by transforming the gaming sector into an interoperable sector through NFTs.    The advent of blockchain technology and decentralized finance in the gaming industry works on that concept by enabling players to capture the utility and value of their gaming experience and asset acquisitions more effectively with NFTs.    NFTs have transformed how we perceive ownership and media. These unique, rare, non-fungible tokens are changing player ownership with their provable scarcity, interoperability, and immutability, driving adoption towards a far more optimized value model.    The revenue created by the global gaming industry is based on three markets: mobile, PC, and console games, which together are worth billions of dollars. However, while the market continues to grow and expand, the players generate little lasting value for themselves.

By spending thousands of dollars on getting the latest hardware for their PCs, getting expensive consoles, or buying the next generation of gaming phones, players entering gaming environments are being offered a tiered-access user experience. This means the more money they spend on their phones, PC hardware, and consoles, the better their experience.    That’s one problem. The next problem is the money flow. In traditional games, money flows in one direction; players have to spend money to unlock in-game content, and exclusive features such as skins, DLCs, and battle passes. Contrarily, blockchain games emphasize generating value for players.    While blockchain games are still developing, they aren’t as visually refined as traditional games; however, they’re setting a standard. That standard introduces a shift that enables players to better capture the utility and value of in-game assets acquired through in-game purchases, gameplay, or other promotional events.    For example, when a player unlocks a certain knife upgrade in a traditional game, their only benefit is enhanced gameplay within the realm of their game. However, in blockchain games, where in-game assets are utilized as cross-platform NFTs, the same knife can be tokenized in a way that evolves in-game purchases into transferable assets that may have the same benefits in other games, or they could be exchanged for money and digital assets.    NFTs are driving the blockchain gaming industry because they’re unique and can be designed to retain value beyond the borders of their original games.
